Frequently Asked Questions

What rivers do you run?

We run a variety of rivers depending on the permits we get from Firsh and Game each year. We do run some local one day trips for those that want a taste of what it is like to run some small rapids.

We apply for the following rivers at minimum each year: Colorado: Yampa & Animus
Idaho: Selway & Lochsa
Oregon: Owyhee & Illinois
California: North Fork American & Kaweah
Arizon: Salt River
New Mexico: Rio Grande
Utah: San Juan & various parts of the Colorado including Cataract Canyon

What time of the year are these runs?

These runs can start as early at mid March and go until late July sometime early August. It all depends on how much rainfall and water there is on the rivers to be able to have a fun and safe trip.

Do I need to be able to swim?

Yes, you must be able to swim and feel comfortable in the water. Even though each person has a life vest, each paddler on a rafting boat has to know how to swim.

Is there a minimum age to participate?

Yes. Depending on the river and how fast the river is flowing will depend on the minimum age. Early in the season it is generally 13 yrs old. Latter in the season it wil 10 yrs old.

What should I wear

For lower body: Swimming wear (or a change of underclothing) to wear under your rafting pants and tennis shoes. These will get wet.

For the upper body: Bring a long sleeved shirt to wear under your rafting jacket (it will get wet), any necessary medications (asthma inhalers, etc.) and a towel to dry off after your trip each day.

For multiple day trips make sure you bring warm cloths such as sweatshirt and pants for the evenings. It can and does get quite cold along the river. Bring a sleeping bag for overnight trips.

We supply all other equipment that you will need including the food.
